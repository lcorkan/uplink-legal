Uplink Privacy Policy

Effective Date: July 18, 2026  ·  Last Updated: July 18, 2026

1. Introduction

Uplink our app that helps Colorado State University ("CSU") students discover, RSVP to, and share campus events. Uplink is currently operated by its founding team; a company entity is in formation. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use and share it, how long we keep it, and the choices you have.

By creating an account or using Uplink (the "Service"), you agree to the practices described here.

Uplink is independent of CSU. We don't receive your official education records from the university, and this Policy — not FERPA — governs the information you give us directly. See Section 9.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Account information and eligibility verification

To sign up, you need a valid CSU email address (@colostate.edu), plus your first and last name, a username, and a password. Your password is hashed by Supabase Auth and never stored in plain text.

Because Uplink is only for current CSU students, we periodically re-verify your eligibility. If you don't re-verify within 365 days, your account is locked until you do. Locking an account doesn't delete it — your data is retained under the same rules as any active account (see Section 5) unless you choose to delete your account yourself.

2.2 Profile information

At signup, you're required to provide your age (entered as a number, not a full date of birth), class year, and major. You may optionally add a bio, a profile photo, and social media handles. Your age is stored but never shown to other users. Your class year and major are visible to other users, as described in Section 4.2. Social media handles, if added, are visible to anyone who views your profile. Uplink does not have a user-to-user "follow" or direct-message feature — you can follow organizer/event pages, not individual people.

2.3 Onboarding information

How you heard about Uplink, and what kinds of events interest you, collected when you first join.

2.4 Content you or organizers post

Only approved organizers can post events. Event posts include photos/videos (via Cloudinary), captions, and details including the event's address — which is shown publicly to all users, even before they RSVP.

2.5 Behavioral and interaction information

RSVPs, saved events, post views/impressions, follows of organizer/event pages, and attendance signals.

2.6 Reports and moderation

Reports or flags you submit about other users or content, and any resulting moderation action. Reports are reviewed by our team. At this stage, our moderation is reactive rather than automated: content goes live immediately when posted, and is reviewed only if someone reports it. We keep the identity of a person who submits a report confidential from the person they reported.

2.7 Organizer and sponsor information

Organization name and details for approved event organizers, generally shown publicly on event listings and organizer pages. Sponsor accounts are currently created by our team directly (an admin-assigned email and password) — we do not currently collect business information from sponsors themselves. If that changes as sponsor features develop, we'll update this section.

2.8 Technical and device information

Session and device data generated by Supabase (login timestamps, device type, general session metadata). Uplink is built to support push notifications through OneSignal, but as of this Policy's effective date, that feature is not active: no push token or any other data is currently sent to OneSignal, and no push notifications are sent to anyone. When we turn this feature on, we'll update this Policy first to describe exactly what's collected and how it's used.

2.9 AI search queries

Uplink's AI search feature sends the text of your query to Google's Gemini API. We are currently using Google's free/unpaid API tier. Under Google's terms for that tier, Google may use the content of your queries and the responses generated to provide, improve, and develop Google's own products, services, and machine-learning technologies — and human reviewers may read, annotate, and process that input and output.

Google's own terms specifically instruct developers not to submit sensitive, confidential, or personal information on this tier. Please don't type anything into Uplink's search bar that you wouldn't want a Google employee to potentially see, or that you wouldn't want used to improve Google's AI products. Treat it like a public search engine, not a private chat.

2.10 Communications

Transactional email (verification codes, password resets, account/admin notifications) via Resend. We don't currently send marketing email.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information above to:

Create/maintain your account and verify your CSU eligibility, including periodic re-verification. Personalize your feed and event recommendations. Power AI search (Section 2.9). Send notifications about events, RSVPs, and account activity, once push notifications are enabled (see Section 2.8). Enable event pages, organizer follows, and other social features. Review reports and enforce our community guidelines. Provide support and respond to your requests. Maintain, secure, and improve Uplink, including fraud/abuse prevention. Send essential service communications.

We do not currently send marketing communications, but we may add optional marketing email or push notifications in the future. If we do, we'll make it opt-in or give you a clear, working unsubscribe option, and we'll update this Policy first.

We do not currently use third-party analytics or crash-reporting tools. If we add any, we'll update this Policy and the vendor list in Section 4.1.

4. How We Share Your Information

We don't sell your personal information, and we don't share it with data brokers or third-party advertisers. We share information only as follows.

4.1 Service providers

Supabase — database, authentication, backend hosting.
Cloudinary — storage/processing for photos and videos.
Google (Gemini API, free tier) — processes AI search query text. See Section 2.9.
Resend — delivers transactional email.
OneSignal — integrated for future push notifications, but not yet active. No data currently flows to OneSignal.

4.2 Other Uplink users

Your username, name, profile photo, bio, class year, major, and social media handles (and posted content) are visible to other users as part of the app's normal functionality. There is no user-to-user following or messaging. Organization details appear on organizer pages; sponsor accounts don't carry additional business information beyond their login. Event addresses are visible to all users, including before RSVP.

4.3 Legal, safety, and non-consensual imagery removal requests

We may disclose information if required by law or if we believe in good faith it's necessary to protect the rights, property, or safety of Uplink, our users, or the public.

Separately, if you believe intimate imagery of you has been posted without your consent, use our dedicated removal-request process at uplink-team@googlegroups.com — handled separately from general content reports. We will review valid requests and remove the content, and any known duplicates, within 48 hours, consistent with the federal TAKE IT DOWN Act.

4.4 Business transfers

If Uplink is acquired, merged, or transfers assets, your information may transfer as part of that transaction, subject to this Policy. If a transaction results in materially different use of your data, we'll notify you first.

5. Data Retention

Active accounts: retained while your account is active, including while locked pending re-verification.

Account deletion: when you delete your account, your personal data and posted content are removed completely. We aim to complete this within 30 days on our production systems; any residual copies in routine backups are cleared on our normal backup cycle.

Moderation records: kept for up to 2 years after resolution, to support repeat-offender tracking, safety enforcement, and legal defense.

AI search queries: governed in part by Google's own retention for the free tier (Section 2.9); on our side we don't separately store your query text longer than needed to show your results.

6. Your Choices and Rights

Access and correction: view/edit most profile information directly in Settings.

Account deletion: delete your account and all associated data anytime from Settings → Delete Account, in-app, no support ticket required.

Notifications: push notifications aren't active in the current version of Uplink. Once enabled, you'll be able to manage them in Settings or your device's system settings.

Data requests: email uplink-team@googlegroups.com to ask what we have about you, request a copy, or ask us to correct or delete it. We aim to respond within 45 days.

All users, regardless of state: Uplink doesn't meet the CCPA or Colorado Privacy Act thresholds yet, but we extend the core rights above to every user as a matter of practice.

7. Children's Privacy and Age Eligibility

Uplink requires a valid CSU email address to sign up, which in practice limits accounts to enrolled students who are virtually always 17 or older. Uplink is not directed at children and is not intended for anyone under 13. We don't knowingly collect personal information from children under 13, and we'll delete any such information promptly if we learn of it.

8. Content You Report and Non-Consensual Imagery

If you see content that violates our community guidelines, you can report it in-app.

If the content is intimate imagery of you posted without your consent — including an AI-generated image or video — use our dedicated removal-request process at uplink-team@googlegroups.com, separate from general content reports; we will remove valid requests, and known duplicates, within 48 hours, consistent with the federal TAKE IT DOWN Act.

9. Relationship to Your University

Uplink is independent of CSU. We are not a "school official" under FERPA, we don't receive your official education records from the university, and this Policy — not FERPA — governs the information you give us directly.

10. Data Security

We use technical and organizational measures designed to protect your information, including encryption in transit, hashed passwords, and vendor access controls. No system is 100% secure. If a breach affects your personal information, we will notify you and, where required, regulators, consistent with applicable law including Colorado's breach notification statute.

11. International Data Transfers

Uplink and its current service providers are primarily U.S.-based. Some providers may process limited data outside the U.S. as part of their own global infrastructure. If Uplink expands outside the U.S., we'll update this section.

12. Changes to This Policy

We'll update the "Last Updated" date above when this Policy changes, and give additional notice — such as an in-app message — before material changes take effect.

13. Contact Us

Questions or requests: uplink-team@googlegroups.com
